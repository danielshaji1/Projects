Wk05d: Activity Quiz:
Flowers & Carrots on a Grid

This exercise provides you with a JPanel that draws a background grid, supports click-to-place flowers in cells, and includes a simple left→right carrot demo at the top so you can see animation wiring.

Your task is to extend it with additional creative and data-structure-oriented features—keeping your code organized, modular, and interesting.

You should be able to complete this in less than one day or in one class session.

TODO Tasks:

  1. Add Variety to Petals (15 pts): Use random or user-selected colors so each flower is unique.
  2. Background Grid Variations (15 pts):
    1. Checkerboard background (parameterize the 2 colors).
    2. Diagonal lines pattern (parameterize number of lines). (Skeleton prints a TODO; you implement.)
  3. Grid-based Flower Placement (15 pts):
    1. Draw flowers in a structured grid (e.g., only even rows and even columns).
    2. Center each flower within its grid cell.
    3. Add slight jitter so they’re not too uniform.
  4. Carrot Mode (5 pts):
    1. When carrot mode is ON, draw a carrot at the cell center instead of a flower. (Skeleton prints a TODO; you implement.)
  5. Falling Flowers (Required for Full Credit) (15 pts):
    1. Replace the demo with an animation where some (or all) flowers fall from the top to the bottom.

In-class progress: 25 points.

Scoring: Core TODOs = 4 × 15 + 5 = 65 pts. With in-class progress (25 pts) the quiz totals 90 pts.

Required to reach 100: Complete 1 Embellishment (10 pts). A second embellishment can earn up to +10 bonus.

Embellishments (choose at least 1 for +10; a second optional for bonus):

  • Chevron or other custom background pattern.
  • Path-following motion for a flower (e.g., circle or rectangle).
  • Extra interaction such as keyboard toggles or tooltips.
  • Any tasteful visual tweak or small effect.

Starter Code Provided

You have two classes in the ZIP:

  • FlowerPanelGrid (a JPanel): Manages the grid, click-to-place flowers, background options, and the small demo carrot.
  • FlowerApplication (the main class): Creates the frame and status bar.

The left→right carrot demo at the top shows a minimal Swing Timer loop. Replace it with your falling flowers animation and any embellishments you choose.

Hint: For a checkerboard, use alternating fillRect calls. For a chevron, you can draw overlapping angled polygons. Feel free to overlay the drawGrid() lines on top of these backgrounds.
